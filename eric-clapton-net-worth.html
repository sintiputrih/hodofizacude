Eric Clapton | Houses, Cars, Guitars & Art Collection

Houses

Legendary musician Eric Clapton has owned numerous properties in different parts of the world, including France, England, the United States, Italy, and Antigua.

His home in Antigua, also known as Standfast Point, was built in 2000 for $14 million. He began renting it in 2009 for $50,000 per week.

Similarly, he also owned a 14,000 square feet beach house in Venice Beach, California.

Cars

If Eric Clapton is known for anything else but his music, then it has to be for his love of Ferraris.

The three times hall of fame inductee is a self-proclaimed car enthusiast, having collected countless numbers of automobiles over the years.

Reportedly, Clapton’s car collection is easily worth $50 million or even $100 million.

The coolest car in his collection has to be the one made especially for him; the Ferrari SP12 EC. Ferrari honored Clapton in 2012 with a one-off special project car which is worth around $4.7 million.

Similarly, some of his other extremely valuable Ferraris are 365 GTC, 1964 Ferrari 250 GT Lusso (1 of 350) potentially worth $10 million today, and Ferrari Enzo (1 of 400) potentially worth $3-5 million.

Moreover, Ferrari 612 Scaglietti, Ferrari 365 GTC, 1991 Ferrari F40, Ferrari 275 GTB, and Ferrari 599 FTB Fiorano are some of his other prized possessions.

Guitars

Eric Clapton is synonyms to guitar. He has played thousands of guitars since he first stepped into the industry.

The guitar played by Clapton has been auctioned for millions of dollars.

Some of his most iconic guitars include Stratocaster “Blackie”, 1964 Gibson ES0335 TDC, 1939 CF Martin & Co, and Gold Leaf Stratocaster.

In addition to that, Clapton used his Fender Stratocaster ‘Brownie’ to record the iconic riff of ‘Layla.’

Art Collection

Just like his love for Ferraris, Clapton also has a thing for art pieces.

Nonetheless, his collection is very difficult to value.

To give you an idea, he once bought $3.4 million worth of three Gerhard Richter paintings in 2001. The three art pieces eventually sold for a combined $77.3 million.

Some other pieces in his collection include “Le Bouquet” by Maurice Utrillo, “Nu Debout” by Henri Matisse and “Le Sommeil” by Edgar Degas.

Eric Clapton | Lifestyle

Though Clapton leads a lavish lifestyle today, he has had his share of bad days in the past.

From alcohol and heroin addiction to the passing away of his four-year-old son, Clapton has seen the worst.

On March 27, 1979, Eric married Pattie Boyd. Boyd was previously married to a member of the Beatles and Eric’s close friend George Harrison.

In fact, Eric wrote the famous song “Layla” about his feelings for Boyd.

The two divorced in 1989.

Clapton has admitted that he used to abuse Boyd and also fathered two children with other women during their marriage.

His daughter was born in 1985, and his son Conor in 1986. Unfortunately, four-year-old Conor passed away, falling out of an open window in a 53rd-floor apartment.

Clapton wrote another famous song, “Tears in Heaven,” in memory of his son.

Moreover, in Jan of 2002, Clapton married Melia McEnery. Together they share three daughters; Sophie, Ella, and Julie.

As we all know, Clapton lived through the drug, sex, and rock n roll era. He also became the victim of drug abuse. Reportedly, Clapton used to spend $16,000 each week on heroin.

Nonetheless, he has been sober and clean since 1987.

Eric Clapton | Charity

Eric Clapton was appointed a director of the UK treatment center for drug and alcohol dependence Clouds House. He also served on their board until 1997.

Eric Clapton | Music, Investments & Books

Music

In 1963, Eric Clapton joined the rock band Yardbirds and stayed with them until March 1965.

He left the band the day when their big hit ‘For You Love’ was released.

Similarly, he then joined John Mayall & the Bluesbreakers and left a few months later. He rejoined the band and quit it again in 1966.

After that, Clapton joined another rock band, Cream. He released six studio albums with Cream. However, Cream disbanded in 1968.

Clapton then formed another band named Blind Faith, which lasted only for seven months.

Following that, Clapton formed another band named Derek and the Dominos.

He released his most famous song Layla along with the band. Nonetheless, it disbanded too in 1971.

He then went on to work with several other bands releasing hit albums and singles.

In 1970, Clapton embarked upon a solo career.

Over his long and illustrious career, Clapton has played more than 3,000 concerts in more than 60 countries.

Clapton retired from worldwide touring in 2015 but still makes music.

He celebrated his 70th birthday with live shows at London’s Royal Albert Hall and New York City’s Madison Square Garden.

Investments

Eric Clapton started Marshbrook Ltd holding company in the early 80s. The company had earned Clapton £110 million since 1989. The current status of the company is unknown.

Similarly, he purchased 50% of gentleman’s outfitters Cordings Piccadilly in 2003.

Book Publications

Eric Clapton has published a handful of books, including his autobiography, over the years.

In 2007 he published his autobiography entitled Clapton: The Autobiography. The book tells the story of his personal as well as professional journeys.

Similarly, in 2012 he published another book titled Six String Stories. This one tells his story through the history of his instruments.

Eric Clapton | Career

Eric Clapton was born in Ripley, Surrey, England, to 16 years old mother Patricia and father Edward, a Canadian soldier.

Clapton never got to meet his father.

Because of his mother’s young age, Eric was told that Patricia was his sister and her mother and father (Eric’s grandparents) were his parents.

His mother left for Germany after marriage.

Clapton learned to play guitar as a teenager. He was so good that he started to play in pubs at the age of 16.

At the age of 17, he joined an R&B group, the Roosters.
