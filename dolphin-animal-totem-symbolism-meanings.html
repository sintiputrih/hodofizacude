Symbolic Meaning Of Dolphin

The Dolphin totem symbol has also made a presence throughout various cultures and mythologies. For example, Celtic symbolism honored dolphins as the guardians and protectors of sacred water.

The Christian religion sees the dolphin as a symbol of Christ. They often associate resurrection with dolphins. They believe that the compassionate, calm demeanor of the dolphin speaks to those who emulate Christ. These are individuals with pure hearts. Some Christian artists have also depicted dolphins transporting the souls of the faithfully departed to heaven.

Similarly, ancient Greeks followed this line of belief. They claim that the animal swam the souls of the dead to the Islands of the Blessed. The Greeks and Romans took the dolphin’s role a step further. They made it an associate of several of the gods. Some of them include Apollo, Aphrodite, Poseidon, Neptune, Eros, Cupid, Dionysus, and Bacchus.

Notably, the dolphin animal totem portrayed certain godly traits. They include fatherly protection, loving, and curiousness. Consequently, the dolphin’s symbolism is an old and respected myth. It continues to be relevant and helpful to our lives today.

When the dolphin totem comes diving across your path, it brings a single, delightful spiritual message; one of joy. They spend a large part of their day messing around with their friends. Thus, they remind you of the importance of doing that sometimes.

Life cannot always be so serious and dull. These beautiful creatures are in complete harmony with their environment.

Dolphin Relations Similar To That Of Humans

Certainly, Dolphins are similar to humans in many ways; unlike almost every other animal in the world, dolphins practice love for each other. This goes beyond basic caregiving for the elderly and the young.

Dolphins have crossed inter-species boundaries to provide assistance, healing, and companionship. This way, they offer major inspiration for humans to treat each other better and become united in love.

African Symbolism Of Dolphins

African people also have some beliefs about animals and the impact they have on their lives. In some African cultures, the Dolphin is believed to accompany the spirits of dead people to the “Islands of The Blessed.”

Legend has it that there was once a man who sought help from pirates who wanted to sell him as a slave. Because he wanted to stay safe, he summoned spirits and magical creatures that scared the pirates, and they jumped into the sea and turned into Dolphins. Dolphins became human friends, protectors, and helpers as a sign of atonement for the sins of their ancestors, the pirates.

It is believed that Dolphins help humans anytime they encounter one drowning. They help them get to shore in a bid to save their lives. Africans believe that the Dolphins are a representation of love.
