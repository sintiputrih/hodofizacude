Cheyenne Emanuel Allegations, What Happened To Caesar Daughter From Black Ink?

Caesar Emmanuel, the owner of Black Ink Crew, has been estranged from his daughter Cheyenne Emmanuel after she accused him of abuse, and fans are curious to know what happened to Caesar’s daughter.

The American reality television series Black Ink Crew (also known as Black Ink Crew: New York) aired on VH1 on January 7, 2013. The success of the show has spawned spin-off series set in Chicago and Compton.

The show has nine seasons and follows the everyday operations and staff drama at a tattoo parlor in New York City’s Harlem area.

The series got renewed for a ninth season on March 8, 2021, which premiered on April 19, 2021. Season nine’s second half premiered on February 21, 2022.

What Happened To Caesar Daughter From Black Ink? Cheyenne Emanuel Allegations

Ceaser Emanuel’s daughter, Cheyenne, accused him of dragging her out of the shower, while naked and beating her “like a dog in the street.”She said her father was drunk when he smashed multiple doors and threw a speaker at her, prompting police involvement.

During Black Ink Crew Season 9 Episode 5 (aired May 18, 2021), Ceaser officially reacted to her charges, claiming that his daughter stayed with him and Suzette during the global lockdown.

When Black Ink Crew returned from hiatus in February 2022, the shop owner held a press conference to share his story, announcing his decision to sue Cheyenne’s mother, Crystal Torres, for character defamation.
