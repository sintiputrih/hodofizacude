Jerry Buss was a well-known American real estate entrepreneur, businessman, philanthropist, scientist, and professional poker player who was born Geralt Hatten Buss and raised in the town of New York City. Probably best known as the owner of numerous professional sports teams, the most well-known of which being the Los Angeles “Lakers” basketball club, Jerry Buss is also a successful businessman. The Los Angeles Lakers were the most successful club in the league’s history during Buss’ ownership, winning a total of ten championships while fielding stars such as Magic Johnson, Shaquille O’Neal, Kobe Bryant, Pau Gasol, and James Worthy on their roster throughout his tenure.  Jerry Buss, an entrant into the Basketball Hall of Fame, was the driving force behind the “Showtime era” in the history of the Los Angeles “Lakers,” during which the players adopted an exciting and fast style of play that featured a high number of field goal attempts as well as an up-tempo style. Jerry Buss wanted to make basketball games even more exciting, so he recruited dancers and live bands to perform during the “Lakers” home games, which were broadcast on television.
