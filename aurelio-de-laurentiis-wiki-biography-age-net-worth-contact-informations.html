BIOGRAPHY OF AURELIO DE LAURENTIIS

Aurelio De Laurentiis, in his long career, he has produced and distributed over 400 films, directed by Mario Monicelli, Carlo Verdone, Ettore Scola, Ridley Scott, David Cronenberg, Luc Besson, Paul Haggis, Joel and Ethan Coen, David Lynch, Roberto Benigni and many others.

As part of the 2010 Venice International Film Festival, he received the “Variety Profile in Excellence” award, attributed to him by the prestigious American magazine Variety with the following motivation: “Aurelio De Laurentiis has always been able to stay in touch with the tastes of the public. He is truly the only Italian producer who still emanates that genuine grandeur given by a symbiotic relationship with a wide audience. Aurelio has an innate radar for pop drives, has business acumen and a real willingness to take risks “. In the United States, the World Guinness of Records awarded him the certificate that recognizes and qualifies the cycle of 30 Christmas comedies, which he himself created, as the longest-running film series in history. In France in 2002, thanks to Aurelio De Laurentiis’ long and important relationship with French cinema, the French Minister of Culture, Jean-Jacques Aillagon, awarded him the title of “Officier de l’Ordre des Arts et des Lettres “. In October 2004, the President of the Republic Carlo Azeglio Ciampi appointed him” Grand Officer of the Republic “. In January 2007, the Mayor Walter Veltroni awarded him the title of “Ambassador of Rome”. In June 2008, he was awarded the title of “Cavaliere del Lavoro” by the President of the Italian Republic, Giorgio Napolitano, who in December of the same year also awarded him the “Leonardo Quality Italy Award”. In October 2010, the Americans awarded him the prestigious “United States-Italy Friendship Award” in Washington. In 2012, in England, the Minister of Trade and Investments Lord Stephen Green of Hustpierpoint, presented him with the “Career Recognition Award”. On 23 May 2014, the American University of Rome awarded him the Laurea Honoris Causa, Doctor of Humane Letters Degree, “for his long and amazing career, and for his contribution to Italian and international enterprise in the field of cinema and sport.”

In his career, with his films, Aurelio De Laurentiis has won more than 50 Gold Tickets for films with the greatest audience attendance, 15 David di Donatello, 7 Golden Globes of the Foreign Press, 7 Silver Ribbons of the National Union of Italian Film Journalists, and many other awards.

From 1993 to 2003 he was President of the World Federation of Producers to which 26 countries belong, from the United States to China, from Japan to South America to Europe.

From October 2001 to June 2006, he was President of the National Union of Film Producers of Anica.

Since 2008, he has been one of the main shareholders of Italian Entertainment Group Spa (which controls, among others, Cinecittà Parchi Spa, Luneur Park Spa and also participates in Filmmaster Group Spa, the company that will organize the opening and closing ceremonies of the Olympics in Rio 2016).

In May 2014, Aurelio De Laurentiis created a working group in Los Angeles for the development and production of American television series. In addition to his love for cinema, De Laurentiis has another great passion, and since 2004 he has started a new career in the world of football. In that same year, he took over the historic club of the Neapolitan city from the Bankruptcy Court of Naples. Forced to restart from Serie C, in just three years the President managed to regain Serie A for his team, and in 27 months (less than 1000 days) he moved from Serie C to European competitions.

Napoli are the only Italian team that in the last 5 years has always participated in European cups, including 2 Champions League, in which they have beaten prestigious teams such as Manchester City, Borussia Dortmund, Olympique Marseille, Chelsea and Arsenal.

In the De Laurentiis era, Napoli won two Italian Cups, against Juventus in 2012 and against Fiorentina in 2014. The victory of the prestigious Super Cup against Juventus also dates back to 22 December 2014.

Thanks to the intelligent and prudent management of the Napoli Soccer Sports Society, Aurelio De Laurentiis was awarded the Football Leader Financial Fair Play 2014 Award, given to him on June 3, 2014: the President was awarded as a “sports manager who has particularly distinguished himself in the shrewd, correct, virtuous and innovative economic management of a football club , in compliance with the new and current rules of financial fair play, becoming a reference model for the European sports scene. ”

With great dedication and constant work, Aurelio De Laurentiis led the Italian club to rank among the top thirty most important teams in the world.
