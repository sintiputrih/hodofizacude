Shohreh Aghdashloo (born May 11, 1952) is an Iranian-American actress. Following numerous starring roles on the stage, her first major film role was in The Report (Gozāresh) (1977) directed by Abbas Kiarostami, which won the Critics Award at the Moscow Film Festival. Her next film was Shatranje Bad (Chess of the Wind), directed by Mohammad Reza Aslani, which screened at several film festivals. Both films were banned in her home country; but, in 1978, Aghdashloo won acclaim for her performance in Sooteh Delan (Broken-Hearted), directed by Ali Hatami, which established her as one of Iran’s leading actresses.

Shohreh Aghdashloo Career

  • As per her career, in 1989, Aghdashloo made her American film debut starring role in Guests of Hotel Astoria.
  • Her television debut came on 25 September 1990 in a guest role in the two-hour episode of the NBC television series Matlock, titled “Nowhere to Turn: A Matlock Mystery Movie”.
  • In 2000, she appeared on screen sporadically, including in the widely panned Surviving Paradise.
  • That film was the first English-language Iranian American feature film released in the United States.
  • In 2001, Aghdashloo was cast opposite Ben Kingsley and Jennifer Connelly in director Vadim Perelman’s House of Sand and Fog.
  • She was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress.
  • In 2008, Aghdashloo served as an official festival judge at the second annual Noor Iranian Film Festival in Los Angeles.
  • In the same year, she also portrayed Sajida Talfah in the HBO original miniseries House of Saddam.
  • She received the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie.
