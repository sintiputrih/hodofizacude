Elaine Starchuk The Ex-Wife Of Tommy Lee

Elaine Starchuk is an Aries and was born on April 4th, 1964 in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. She is 55 years old.

The American is most known as a model and, among other things, as the ex-wife of drummer for Mötley Crüe, Tommy Lee. She lived in Hugh Heffner’s Playboy Penthouse while working as a Playboy model.

Their union was brief, and following their divorce, Elaine’s fame started to slowly fade away since she began devoting more of her time to her family and other essential aspects of her life.

After stepping away from the spotlight, she turned to teach, but she had to take a pause after suffering an accident in 2016.

Is Elaine Starchuk Married? Relationship

Starchuk wed Tommy Lee in 1984, but the couple split up after just seven days for unexplained reasons and got divorced the following year. Despite her having four children, later on, they never had any children together.

A year after divorcing Starchuk, Tommy’s second romance was with Heather Locklear. After divorcing Locklear, Tommy Lee temporarily became engaged to model Bobbie Brown; however, they never wed.

Instead, Tommy started dating actress and model Pamela Anderson, with whom he had two children: son Brandon Thomas in 1996 and son Dylan in late 1997.

They divorced in 1998 nonetheless, allegedly because Anderson was holding their younger kid when Lee allegedly kicked her.

Despite the fact that their relationship had ended because of it, Lee and Anderson briefly rekindled their romance after Tommy was released from prison after serving a six-month sentence. However, they split up once more in 2001.

Although it was claimed that they were back together in 2008, their relationship experienced further ups and downs before they finally called it quits in the following year.

A singer with German and Greek background named Sofia Toufa and Lee became engaged in 2014; however, their engagement ended in 2017.

The most recent is that he wed social media personality, Brittany Furlan, in 2019.

How did Elaine Starchuk start her Professional Career?

Along with Vince Neil, Nikki Sixx, Mick Mars, and others, Lee was a founding member of the rock band Mötley Crüe. The band has sold over 100 million CDs and received multiple platinum and gold certifications on its way to becoming a global superstar.

The band’s debut album, “Too Fast for Love,” featuring the songs “Live Wire,” “Piece of Your Action,” and “Stick to Your Guns,” was released in 1981.

While it received generally favorable reviews from critics and fans, Mötley Crüe was referred to as “a bash-’em-out bar band, making up in enthusiasm what they lack in technical skill” on allmusic.com.

With a lot on their plate, they put out “Shout at the Devil” in 1983, which included songs like “Looks That Kill” and “Helter Skelter,” and “Theatre of Pain” in 1985.

Together with Tom Werman, they released “Girl, Girls, Girls” in 1987, their fourth studio album, which featured the hits “Wild Side” and “Girls, Girls, Girls.” After selling more than four million copies, the album went on to receive four platinum certifications.

Accident

In 2016, a Vancouver-based Langley deli was struck by an SUV that struck Starchuk. The automobile was reportedly being driven by an elderly guy who lost control of it.

According to witnesses, the former model was forced against the deli wall, which crumbled around her. She was in pain, gasping for air, and thought her ribs were shattered.

Elaine Starchuk was taken to Royal Columbian Hospital, where she checked herself out despite having sore hands, arms, and neck. She received more than $400,000 in compensation for the following period.

Ex-Husband

Tommy Lee is best known as the drummer of the band Mötley Crüe. He was born Thomas Lee Bass on 3 October 1962 in Athens, Greece.

His parents are American David Lee Thomas Bass, a former sergeant in the US Army, and his Greek mother Vassiliki Papadimitriou, a model and 1960 Miss Greece hopeful.

Tommy’s family relocated to Los Angeles when he was a year old, where he spent his formative years.

Following Career

The band worked on “Dr. Feelgood,” an album with 11 songs that included “Don’t Go Away Mad (Just Go Away),” “Same Ol’ Situation (S.O.S. ),” and “Dr. Feelgood,” among others, in 1989.

They released an album with the same name in 1994, followed by “Generation Swine,” their seventh studio album, three years later.

After the failure of their previous album, Mötley Crüe was under pressure to create an album that would get noticed; nevertheless, “Generation Swine” only earned fair reviews.

They released “New Tattoo” in 2000, and “Saints of Los Angeles,” their most recent album, with songs like “Face Down in the Dirt,” “What’s It, Gonna Tak,” and “This Ain’t a Love Song.”

After the band broke up, Lee started a career in the rap metal genre and created the group Methods of Mayhem.

In addition to his solo projects over the years, he also collaborated with Ludacris in 2010 and Smashing Pumpkins in 2014, the same year that Motley Crue played their final shows before signing a contract promising never to use the name again.

He had a brief literary career, contributing to a joint biography of Mötley Crüe.
