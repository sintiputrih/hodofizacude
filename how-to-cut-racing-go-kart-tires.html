How do you make homemade tire softener?

Fortunately, making tire softener is an extremely simple process. Mix together a solution composed of 1/3 acetone, 1/3 mineral spirits and 1/3 transmission fluid. Be sure to create mixture in a well-ventilated area. Apply mixture to tires using rag or a paint roller.

Why are my tires dry rotting?

What is Tire Dry Rot? Tires naturally degrade over a period of time because they’re made from rubber. When a tire ages, it loses the protective resin which keeps the rubber from oxidizing and drying out. As those oils evaporate, the tire becomes brittle, and starts developing breaks and begins to break apart.

Does tire shine cause tires to break?

Most DIYers will use water or solvent-based tire dressing to provide a shine on tires. They are sticky liquids, often clear in appearance, and contain petroleum solvents. While they make the tire look great, they can lead to premature cracking or drying of the sidewall.

How many years does it take for tires to dry rot?

In arid climates, tire dry rot can set in after as little as five years, while more humid environments aren’t as conducive to the splintering and breaking tire dry rot causes. Can tires go bad from sitting too long?

Your wheels carry the full weight of the car, so when your vehicle is left in one stationary position, your tires can soon become deflated, flat, rotten, or warped. While inflating the tires can help, it’s not always safe to drive on tires that sat in the garage for too long.
