Bruno Tonioli is an Italian choreographer, ballroom dancer, Latin dancer, and TV personality who is distinguished as a judge on the dance show Strictly Come Dancing and its American adaptation Dancing with the Stars. He is also noted for the co-creation of the dance reality shows DanceX and the American adaptation titled Dance War: Bruno vs. Carrie Ann. With time, he has amassed a huge fan base with more than 350k followers on Instagram and more than 400k followers on Twitter.

    Bruno Tonioli Facts

  • He grew up in Ferrara, Italy.
  • It was his parents’ dream to see their son work at a bank or become an account. However, even as a child, he wanted to enter the show biz.
  • He was 18 years old when he left his home in pursuit of becoming a professional dancer. Tonioli relocated to Paris and joined a dance troupe there.
  • His father worked as a bus driver and also painted cars while his mother was a seamstress for car upholstery.
  • Growing up, he faced a lot of bullying due to his sexual orientation.
  • As a child, Tonioli enjoyed watching musicals and looking at art.
  • He was a favorite amongst the ladies because of his impressive dance moves. However, that meant that he had many enemies as well.
  • In an interview, he once stated that he was chased down by a group of boys who held a broken bottle to him while they pinned him against the wall. Luckily, he managed to escape.
  • In 1983, Bruno was featured in legendary singer and songwriter Elton John’s music video for I’m Still Standing.
  • Tonioli has choreographed dance sessions for distinguished singers and songwriters such as Elton John, Duran Duran, The Rolling Stones, Freddie Mercury, Tina Turner, and Paul McCartney.
  • Bruno choreographed the films Ella Enchanted, The Gathering Storm, and What a Girl Wants.
  • As of January 2020, e has an estimated earning of £200,000 to £249,000 as a contributor to the BBC.
  • Bruno was nominated for an Emmy award in 2006 and again in 2007.
  • He is a multilingual person who can speak fluently in English, Spanish, French, German, and Italian.
  • It was his witty personality that helped the American adaption titled Dancing with the Stars rise up to be one of the top shows of ABC.
  • Bruno is close friends with choreographer Arlene Phillips.
