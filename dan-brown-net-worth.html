Dan Brown | Career

After graduation, Dan Brown engaged in music, making effects with a synthesizer and also self-produced “SynthAnimals,” a children’s cassette that sold a few hundred copies.

In 1991, Brown moved to Los Angeles to pursue a singer-songwriter career and taught Spanish classes to support.

Dan decided to return to New Hampshire and took a job as an English teacher at Phillips Exeter Academy in 1993.

Dan was motivated to become a thriller writer after reading Sidney Sheldon’s novel “The Doomsday Conspiracy.”

He co-wrote a novel, “187 Men to Avoid: A Survival Guide for the Romantically Frustrated Woman,” with his wife.

Brown left teaching in 1996 to pursue a career as a full-time writer. In 1998, his novel, “Digital Fortress” was released.

“Angels and Demons” and ‘Deception Point‘ were his next works.

After publishing his masterwork “The Da Vinci Code” in 2003, he became famous. On the first day, it sold about 6,000 copies.
