What movies did Andrew McCarthy direct? Are James Spader and Andrew McCarthy friends?

Andrew Thomas McCarthy, recognized for his multifaceted career spanning acting, travel writing, and directing, has left an indelible mark on the entertainment industry. Amidst his notable roles as part of the Brat Pack in the ’80s, McCarthy has seamlessly transitioned into the director’s chair, showcasing his artistic prowess behind the camera.

Movies Directed by Andrew McCarthy

While Andrew McCarthy first gained prominence as an actor in beloved ’80s films, his directorial ventures have added a new dimension to his artistic repertoire. McCarthy, known for his work on the hit series “Orange Is the New Black,” has successfully helmed several episodes, contributing to the show’s compelling narrative.

Beyond the small screen, McCarthy directed episodes for the popular CW series “Gossip Girl,” displaying his ability to capture the essence of complex characters and intricate storylines. His foray into directing continued with the USA Network’s “White Collar,” where McCarthy not only showcased his acting skills but also left his directorial imprint on the series.

One notable highlight in McCarthy’s directorial career is his involvement in the NBC hit television show “The Blacklist.” Starring James Spader and Megan Boone, McCarthy directed three episodes during the show’s second season, solidifying his position as a director capable of navigating intense and suspenseful narratives.

James Spader and Andrew McCarthy: A Reunion of Old Friends

Fans have long been curious about the off-screen relationships between Hollywood stars, and the connection between James Spader and Andrew McCarthy is no exception. McCarthy recently shared a nostalgic moment on social media, providing a glimpse into the enduring friendship between the two actors.

In a heartwarming Instagram post, McCarthy shared a snapshot of himself and James Spader donning sunglasses and posing against the backdrop of a bustling city. Accompanying the photo was a caption that offered insights into the enduring bond between the two, transcending both time and distance.

“My old friend James Spader. We met in ’85, did three movies together (Pretty in Pink, Mannequin, Less Than Zero) and lost touch for 20 years. For the past decade, I’ve been dropping in often to direct his show, Blacklist,” McCarthy shared.

This revelation not only speaks to the longstanding connection between the two actors but also highlights McCarthy’s role behind the scenes in directing episodes of “The Blacklist.” As fans revel in the nostalgia of their on-screen collaborations and the enduring friendship that has weathered the test of time, McCarthy’s dual contributions to the entertainment world continue to captivate audiences.

As Andrew McCarthy’s career continues to evolve, both as a director and an actor, fans eagerly anticipate the next chapter in his journey—a journey that intertwines the threads of cinematic storytelling and enduring friendships.
