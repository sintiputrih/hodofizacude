Ricki Lake's rocky road to happiness: As actress proudly shows off her 30LB weight loss, FEMAIL lift

Ricki Lake has never been one to shy away from sharing her personal life with her fans, laying bare everything from her childhood sexual assault trauma and battle with obesity to her alopecia diagnosis. 

And now, the 55-year-old has candidly opened up about a much more positive step forward in her life – her staggering 30lb weight loss transformation.  

The Hairspray actress, who rose to stardom in 1988, recently unveiled her incredible her svelte new figure while shutting down suggestion that she’d been taking Ozempic by vehemently denying that her makeover was the result of any pharmaceutical intervention. 

However, the actress’s journey to confidence and self-love hasn’t come easy, with Ricki having gone through many ups and downs due to her yo-yoing weight loss, and her ‘secret’ battle with androgenic hair loss.

Ricki Lake (seen left in 2023 and right on Sunday) wowed her fans this weekend when she proudly showcased her 30lb weight loss on the red carpet at an event in LA 

The 55-year-old Hairspray star looked happy and confident as she posed for the cameras – but her journey to achieving that self love has been anything but easy 

Ricki’s journey to embracing her body and her appearance began at an incredibly tender age – after a childhood sexual assault sparked a complex and lengthy struggle with overeating. 

Through the years, she has opened up candidly about her yo-yoing weight, appearing on the cover of magazines to celebrate a slim-down, while speaking out about her struggles to maintain it. 

Add to that a recent battle with alopecia – which saw Ricki bravely baring her bald head for the world – and it’s clear that the TV star’s journey to self love has been anything but easy.  

Now, FEMAIL has laid bare Ricki’s wild transformations throughout the years – from how she became a ‘role model for heavy people’ to being accused of being a ‘sell out’ when she shed off pounds to having to shave her head due to her challenging fight with alopecia. 

From childhood abuse to stardom and back to rock bottom: How being a victim of sexual abuse at age six caused the actress to develop eating issues

The actress rose to stardom in 1988 (seen) when she appeared in Hairspray, but years before she made it big in Hollywood, she struggled with plenty of childhood trauma

 Ricki (seen in 2001) has gone through many ups and downs due to her yo-yoing weight loss

Ricki began struggling with her weight and eating issues when she was just a child. 

She chalked up her weight gain to the ‘sexual abuse’ she endured as a child at the age of six. 

She told ABC: ‘I was sexually abused as a child, as a young child. And I think that is what contributed to my being overweight… and obese, in the later years. 

 ‘I, even subconsciously, did not want to be attractive to men. And it was my way of, you know, protecting myself, I think… and maybe stuffing my feelings with food.’

By the time she was 10, her weight began creeping up on her. 

She added: ‘I was still kind of cute, so I could pull it off, you know. Through my adolescence and, you know, later teenage years, I got bigger and bigger and bigger.’

She explained that she also had ‘the worst eating habits’ because her family didn’t sit down for dinner.  

Ricki has previously told GMA that as a child, she was a ‘secret eater’ and would often sneak food into her room.

By the time she was in college, Ricki weighed 200 pounds, but it was at 19 that her weight began to benefit her career. 

She soared to fame when she played the leading lady Tracy Turnblad in the first film adaptation of Hairspray. 

To the world, Ricki became known as a ‘pleasantly plump’ girl-next-door who had just had her big break. 

And soon enough, Ricki became a total star, with people begging her to star in various hits such as China Beach and Cry Baby, where she played Johnny Depp’s sister. 

However, the ‘endless flow of opportunities,’ soon came crumbling down when her weight soared to 260 pounds.  According to GMA, Ricki’s agent stopped returning her calls and for a short amount of time, the actress was even homeless. 

It was at this point, Ricki felt like she hit rock bottom and decided that she needed to make a change. However, the actress went to extreme levels to shed off weight – adding that she would never recommend it to anyone else. 

She began starving herself and exercising daily until she dropped from a size 24 to a size 12 and shed off 100 pounds in just over six months.  She told the outlet: ‘I was fainting on, like, the spot. I’d be standing up and then, black out, and not remember anything.’

However, her hair loss battle on top her weight issues made for a complicated situation. 

In 2022, she spoke out about her complicated relationship with weight loss and discussed going on a ‘crash diet’ and getting down to a ‘sample size.’

‘I was offered four magazine covers. It was People, Us Weekly, OK! — the power of that, it was unbelievable. Well, not unbelievable, totally believable, but it happened to me. Yes, I got more opportunities, more attention, it was way more positive,’ she said on her podcast Raised By Ricki.

‘I’m conflicted because I am proud of myself. When I lose that weight, it is something I want the world to know, I did this. It’s a lot of work and a lot of willpower, so I want to talk about it, but I know that it’s not the best thing to be celebrating.’

She said at the time that she was ‘perpetuating that obsession we have with diet culture,’ adding, ‘I’m a part of it.’

In 2023, she took to Instagram to reveal she and her husband Ross Burningham each lost 30 pounds in four months — putting Ricki at 140 pounds now — and did it ‘without relying on a pharmaceutical.’

She added that her weight loss journey took place while she is in perimenopause – the transition period before menopause, which can slow down metabolism.

‘Hi friends. I’ve been wanting to share with you what I’ve been up to these last 4 months. On October 26th, 2023 I made a commitment to myself to get healthier. My husband, Ross joined me in this effort,’ she wrote in the caption

‘Together we have each lost 30+ lbs. I really want to share, because we did this without relying on a pharmaceutical. (Not that there is anything wrong with that.),’ she shared.

‘But neither of us were pre diabetic and both of us felt like we wanted to at least try and do it on our own,’ the star — whose highest weight was 260 pounds — went on.

‘Being 55 and in perimenopause, I was a bit worried, that my body would not drop the lbs like it had in the past.’

‘I am so so proud of us. I feel amazing. I feel strong. I will go in depth in another post of what I did exactly, but suffice to say this is the healthiest way I’ve lost weight in all of my years.’
